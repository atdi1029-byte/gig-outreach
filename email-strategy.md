## 2. INSTAGRAM DM LIMITS

*Instagram does NOT publish official numbers. These are consensus figures from outreach practitioners and community testing.*

### Daily DM Limits (to non-followers — this is your case)

| Account Age | DMs to Non-Followers/Day | DMs to Followers/Day |
|---|---|---|
| New (0-30 days) | 20-30 | 50-70 |
| Growing (30-180 days) | 40-60 | 80-120 |
| Established (180+ days) | 60-100 | 150-200 |

### Hourly Limits

| Account Type | Per Hour (non-followers) |
|---|---|
| New account | 5 |
| Established | 8 |

**Critical:** Sending 10+ messages in a 5-minute burst is far more dangerous than 60 spread across a day. Pacing matters more than total count.

### New Conversations Per Day (Real Bottleneck)

Every venue DM is a new conversation. This is stricter than total DM limits:

| Account Age | Safe New Conversations/Day |
|---|---|
| New (0-30 days) | 10-15 |
| Growing (30-180 days) | 15-25 |
| Established (180+ days) | 25-40 |

### Where Cold DMs Land
- **Message Requests folder** — NOT the main inbox
- Many business accounts don't regularly check Message Requests
- Open rate for cold DMs: ~10-20% (much lower than email)
- Only 1 message request allowed per non-follower — if they don't respond, do NOT message again

### What Triggers Instagram Restrictions
1. 10+ messages in rapid succession (minutes)
2. Identical or near-identical messages to multiple accounts
3. Including links (especially in first messages)
4. High rate of recipients marking you as spam/blocking
5. Messaging accounts with zero prior interaction (no likes, comments, follows)
6. Third-party automation tools (detected within hours)
7. Low acceptance rate on message requests

### Instagram Warm-Up Schedule

| Phase | Timeline | Actions | DM Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Setup | Days 1-3 | Profile photo, bio, 3-6 performance posts. NO DMs. | 0 |
| Engagement | Days 4-7 | Like 15-25 venue posts/day, comment on 5-10. Post 1 clip. | 0 |
| First DMs | Week 2 | DM people who follow you or you've interacted with | 5-10/day |
| Cold DMs | Week 3 | Begin DMs to venues (non-followers) | 10-15/day |
| Scaling | Week 4 | Increase by 5/week if no restrictions | 15-20/day |
| Steady state | Month 2+ | Gradual increase, max +10 per week | 20-40/day |

**If your account is already established (months of posts/followers), start at Week 3.**

### What Happens When You Hit Limits

| Severity | Duration | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Soft limit (delayed delivery) | 1-6 hours | Too many in an hour |
| Action block (can't send DMs) | 24-48 hours | Exceeded daily limit |
| Repeat offense block | 3-7 days | Multiple blocks in short period |
| Temporary restriction | 7-14 days | Repeated violations, mass spam reports |
| Permanent ban | Indefinite | Severe/repeated violations |

Each successive violation gets longer. First block = 24 hours. Second within a week = 3-7 days. Third = 14+ days.

### Instagram DM Best Practices
1. **No links in first message** — #1 spam trigger. Send links only after they reply.
2. **Personalize every message** — reference the venue by name, mention a specific post or event
3. **Vary your messages** — prepare 5-10 template variations, customize each
4. **Engage before you DM** — like 2-3 of their posts first, maybe leave a comment
5. **Keep it short** — 3-4 sentences max (character limit is 1,000 but aim for 300-500)
6. **Content-rich profile** — venues WILL check your profile. Have performance videos, gig photos
7. **Space messages 5-10 min apart minimum**
8. **Only DM once** — if they don't respond to Message Request, don't send another
9. **NO automation tools** — Instagram's 2025-2026 detection catches bots within hours
10. **Avoid spam words:** "free," "deal," "offer," "limited time," "click here"

### Business/Creator Account
- DM limits are the same regardless of account type (based on age + trust score)
- Business/Creator gives you Insights (see who viewed your profile) — recommended
- Looks more professional to venues

## 3. FACEBOOK MESSAGE LIMITS

### Personal Profile Limits (Cold Messages to Non-Friends)

| Account Age | Safe Daily Limit |
|---|---|
| New (0-3 months) | 10-15 |
| Moderate (3-12 months) | 20-30 |
| Established (1+ years) | 30-50 |

**Burst limit:** ~10 messages in a few minutes triggers anti-spam regardless of daily total.

### Where Cold Messages Land
- **Message Requests folder** — NOT the main inbox
- Recipient sees: "[Name] wants to connect with you on Messenger"
- They must Accept or Decline before conversation proceeds
- Open rate: ~20-40% (better than Instagram, worse than email)
- Response rate for cold messages: ~5-10%

### Facebook Business Pages — NOT Useful for Cold Outreach
- Pages can only message people who messaged YOUR page first (24-hour window)
- Cold outreach from Pages is essentially blocked
- **Use your personal profile**, not a business page

### Friend Request Strategy
Friending venue owners before messaging dramatically improves visibility, but has its own limits:

| Account Age | Safe Friend Requests/Day |
|---|---|
| New (0-3 months) | 5-10 |
| Moderate (3-12 months) | 10-20 |
| Established (1+ years) | 20-30 |
| Hard max | ~1,000 pending (unaccepted) total |

**Warning:** If people click "I don't know this person" on your FR, Facebook restricts you fast. Having mutual friends helps.

### What Triggers Facebook Restrictions
1. 10+ rapid messages in succession
2. Multiple recipients marking messages as spam
3. Identical copy-paste messages (detected)
4. High ratio of unaccepted message requests
5. Sending FR then immediately messaging
6. New accounts messaging many non-friends
7. Suspicious links
8. Third-party automation tools

### Facebook Warm-Up Schedule

| Week | Friend Requests/Day | Cold Messages/Day | Other |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1-2 | 0-5 | 0 | Post content, join local groups, engage |
| Week 3-4 | 5-10 | 3-5 | Comment on venue posts |
| Week 5-6 | 10-15 | 5-10 | Start cold messaging venues |
| Week 7-8 | 15-20 | 10-15 | Steady state |

**If your account is established (years old, active), skip to Week 5-6.**

### What Happens When You Get Flagged

| Offense | Consequence |
|---|---|
| 10+ rapid messages | Temp messaging block (hours) |
| Repeated rapid messaging | 24-hour block |
| Multiple spam reports | 3-7 day restriction |
| Identical messages | 1-7 day restriction |
| High FR rejection rate | FR blocked 7-30 days |
| Repeated violations | 30-day "Facebook Jail" |
| Severe violations | Permanent ban |

### Facebook Best Practices
1. **Personalize every message** — never copy-paste identical text
2. **Don't message immediately after friend requesting** — wait at least a few hours, ideally a day
3. **Use sparingly** — Facebook is the riskiest channel for cold outreach
4. **Engage first** — like/comment on their posts before messaging
5. **Keep it short and professional** — 3-4 sentences
6. **Space messages 3-5 minutes apart minimum**
7. **No automation tools**

## 4. EMAIL — Anti-Spam Best Practices

### SPF/DKIM/DMARC
- **For @gmail.com: Google handles all of this automatically.** Nothing to configure.
- Only matters if you move to a custom domain (Google Workspace)

### Email Content Rules
**DO:**
- Keep under 150 words
- Plain text (not HTML templates)
- Personalize first line (reference their venue specifically)
- 0-1 links max (YouTube playlist OR website, not both)
- Include physical address (CAN-SPAM requirement)
- Include opt-out line

**DON'T:**
- ALL CAPS or excessive !!!
- Spam words: "free", "act now", "limited time", "click here"
- Link shorteners (bit.ly, tinyurl)
- Images in cold emails
- Same email body to multiple people
- Send 50 emails in 5 minutes (space 3-5 min apart)

### Subject Lines
**Good:**
- "Live classical guitar for [Venue Name] events?"
- "Quick question about live music at [Venue Name]"
- "[Name], do you book live musicians?"

**Bad:**
- "AMAZING OFFER — Live Music!!!"
- "You won't believe this incredible musician"
- "Re: Our conversation" (deceptive — never talked)

## 9. CAN-SPAM Compliance (Email)

**Required in every outreach email:**
1. Accurate From/Reply-To headers
2. Non-deceptive subject line
3. Physical mailing address (PO Box works)
4. Opt-out mechanism

**Add to email footer:**
```
Alex Barnett | Classical Guitar
Pasadena, MD 21122

Don't need live music? Just reply "no thanks" and I'll remove you from my list.
```

**Penalty:** Up to $51,744 per violating email (but enforcement targets mass spammers, not solo freelancers)

## 14. The #1 Thing That Matters

At your volume, **spam filters aren't the problem — reply rate is.**

10 deeply personalized messages > 100 generic ones.

"I saw your venue hosts jazz nights on Thursdays and thought classical guitar could be a great fit for your Sunday brunch crowd" will outperform a template 5:1.

**Platform priority: Email > Instagram > Facebook.** Email is safest, highest volume, most professional. Instagram lets you show your work visually. Facebook is supplementary.

---

## 15. Red Flags to Avoid (All Platforms)

1. **Copy-paste identical messages** — all platforms detect this
2. **Links in first contact** — especially on Instagram. Email signature link is fine.
3. **Rapid-fire sending** — space 3-5 min apart on every channel
4. **Messaging right after following/friending** — wait hours, ideally a day
5. **Ignoring responses** — reply quickly, platforms track response rate
6. **Weekend blasts** — venue staff busy, looks bot-like
7. **Second message if no response** — on IG/FB, one shot only. Email gets follow-ups.
8. **Automation tools for IG/FB** — manual only, bots get caught fast
