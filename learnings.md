# Gig Outreach Learnings

What works for Alex as a classical guitarist, learned from 18 past gigs and venue reviews.

## The Ideal Venue Profile
- **Wealthy, educated, cultured audience** — this is the #1 factor. Alex's best gigs have audiences who are intelligent, receptive, and genuinely appreciate classical guitar
- **Upscale but not stuffy** — historic buildings, mountain wineries, fine dining restaurants in affluent areas
- **Good tips** — the best venues produce multiple $50 tips. Tips correlate with audience wealth/culture
- **Rebookable** — venues that bring Alex back regularly are the most valuable. Monthly or weekly > one-off
- **Beautiful setting/atmosphere** — architecture, views, acoustics matter. "Every part of being there is amazing"

## What Makes a 9+ Gig
- **University Club (9.5)**: DC private club, extremely intelligent cultured audience, played every Saturday, historic building. Lost contact after manager change. Dream gig.
- **Inn at Perry Cabin (9.5)**: Very upscale hotel restaurant. Great pay, great tips, amazing clientele.
- **L'Auberge Chez Francois (9.3)**: Perfect match. Educated wealthy audience. Top tier but doesn't hire as much anymore.
- **Big Cork Vineyards (9.3)**: Beautiful mountain winery, great architecture, wonderful audience, very good pay/tips, rebooked monthly.
- **Good Spirit Farm Winery (9.0)**: Great pay/tips, rebooked monthly, lovely smart engaged audience, beautiful mountain setting.

### Pattern: 9+ gigs share these traits
1. Audience is educated/cultured/wealthy
2. Setting is beautiful or historic
3. Pay AND tips are both strong
4. Regular rebooking (monthly+)

## What Makes a 7-8 Gig (Solid, Worth Doing)
- **Royal Restaurant (8.8)**: Casual but attracts upscale wealthy educated crowd in Alexandria VA. Insane tips. Weekly gig. Proof that restaurants work when the neighborhood is right.
- **Windridge Vineyards (8.5)**: Good pay/tips, rebooked several times/year, nice educated audience, pretty room with views.
- **Elkridge Furnace Inn (8.3)**: Upscale historic inn, limited seating, incredible audience — intelligent, receptive. Tips nice but rebooking only so-so.
- **Savage Mill Mall (8.0)**: Old mill with great acoustics, good pay/tips, rebooked during holidays. Good crowd.
- **Black Ankle Vineyards (7.8)**: Higher end winery, great architecture, intimate educated crowd. Good pay + great tips.
- **Xella Winery (7.8)**: Good pay/tips, audience surprisingly nice and engaged. Small but pretty.
- **Twin Oaks Tavern Winery (7.8)**: Ok tips, audience not usual crowd but worked. Incredible atmosphere.

## What Makes a Below-Average Gig (Avoid These Patterns)
- **The Farmhouse Tuscan (3.3)**: Low across the board. No notes but clearly wrong fit.
- **Chesapeake Manor Vineyard (6.0)**: Nothing standout. Rarely rebooked. Audience hit or miss. Average.
- **Williams Gap Vineyard (6.5)**: Good pay but not many tips. Only played once. Audience just ok.
- **Hello Betty Bethesda (6.8)**: Low audience score (5), middling tips (6). Nice venue quality though.

### Pattern: Bad gigs share these traits
1. Audience doesn't match — wrong crowd for classical guitar
2. Low tips = wrong demographic
3. No rebooking = they didn't see value in live classical music
4. "Hit or miss" audience = inconsistent venue

## Category Insights
- **Wineries**: Best category overall. 7 out of 18 gigs. Range from 6.0 to 9.3. Mountain wineries with good architecture score highest. Small/unknown wineries can be duds.
- **Restaurants**: Work great IF the neighborhood is wealthy/educated (Alexandria, Bethesda area). Casual restaurants in wrong areas = bad audience. Fine dining = good.
- **Country/Private Clubs**: Highest floor — audience is always cultured. Tips may be limited by club policy. Risk: losing contact after manager changes.
- **Hotels**: Only one data point (Inn at Perry Cabin = 9.5) but upscale hotels with restaurants seem ideal.
- **Malls**: Savage Mill worked because it's historic with good acoustics. Probably wouldn't generalize to generic malls.

## Geographic Insights
- Alexandria VA (Royal Restaurant) = great area, wealthy cultured crowd
- DC (University Club, Maryland Club) = dream territory for private clubs
- Mountain wineries (Big Cork, Good Spirit Farm) = best winery experiences
- Eastern Shore MD (Inn at Perry Cabin) = upscale hotel territory

## Key Signals When Evaluating a New Venue
**Green flags:**
- 100+ Google reviews with 4.5+ stars
- Located in known affluent area
- "Fine dining", "upscale", "historic" in description
- Mountain/vineyard setting with good architecture
- Private club or members-only
- Contact form mentions "private events" or "live entertainment"

**Red flags:**
- Under 50 Google reviews (too small/unknown)
- "Casual dining", "sports bar", "pub" vibes
- Located in non-affluent area
- No social media presence (not invested in experience)
- Owner-operated tiny venues (hit or miss on rebooking)
